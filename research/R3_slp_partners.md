# R3 — Israeli Hebrew clinical SLP ecosystem

Verified map of academic partners, hospitals, validated clinical scales, regulatory body, and the AphasiaBank Hebrew gap.

---

## 1. Naama Friedmann lab (TAU)

Prof. Naama Friedmann is active and heads the **Language and Brain Lab** at TAU's School of Education and the Sagol School of Neuroscience; she holds the Branco Weiss Chair in child development.

- Focus: neuropsychology of language, syntactic and lexical disorders in aphasia, acquired and developmental dyslexia/dysgraphia, language deficits after critical-period deprivation.
- Recent Hebrew aphasia work includes Keret et al. 2023 on Primary Progressive Aphasia in Hebrew speakers.
- Public page: [en-sagol.tau.ac.il/researchers/Naama-Friedmann](https://en-sagol.tau.ac.il/researchers/Naama-Friedmann)
- Lab site: [language-brain.com](http://language-brain.com) (live)
- No public email on the Sagol page snapshot — contact via TAU Sagol office.

## 2. Other Israeli SLP / Communication Disorders departments

**Verified BA programs (CHE-recognized):**

- **TAU** — since 1967, oldest; "Stanley Steyer School of Health Professions". Thesis MA + PhD.
- **University of Haifa** — since 2002, "Department of Communication Sciences and Disorders". Thesis MA + PhD.
- **Hadassah Academic College Jerusalem** — since 2002, MA without thesis.
- **Ono Academic College** — private, since 2007.
- **Ariel University** — since 2009; Bruckner Center for Autism research; Dr. Michal Icht is here.

**Notable exclusions/clarifications:**

- **Bar-Ilan** does NOT offer a Communication Disorders degree but runs an MA in "Linguistics in Clinical Research" (Dept. of English Lit. & Linguistics) targeting CD graduates; the Gonda Multidisciplinary Brain Research Center is the relevant brain-research hub.
- **BGU** — Department of Communication Studies is media-focused; no clinical SLP program found. Linguistics dept. covers neurolinguistics/language disorders coursework only.
- **HUJI: NO Communication Disorders department confirmed** — only Linguistics and Hebrew Language departments. Prior intel was correct.

## 3. Hebrew clinical scales

- **WAB-Hebrew:** Adapted by **Kasher, Batori, Soroker, Graves & Zaidel (1999)**. No published WAB-**R** Hebrew validation surfaced; treat WAB-R Hebrew as **unconfirmed**.
- **BDAE Hebrew:** No formal validated Hebrew adaptation surfaced — **unconfirmed**.
- **FDA-2 Hebrew (dysarthria):** Validated by **Icht, Mama, et al., 2022**, IJLCD; Ariel University. 116 words / 50 sentences; healthy + acquired + developmental dysarthria cohorts. ([DOI](https://doi.org/10.1111/1460-6984.12737))
- **Friedmann-lab batteries** (Hebrew, widely used in Israeli aphasia research):
  - **BAFLA** (Battery for Agrammatism)
  - **FriGvi** (Friedmann–Gvion phonological STM battery, 2002)
  - **SHEMESH** (100-picture naming)
  - **HIFIL** (verb-morphology)
- A separate Hebrew Naming Test independent of SHEMESH was not located — **unconfirmed**.

## 5. Hospitals

- **Loewenstein Rehabilitation Hospital (Ra'anana, Clalit)** — Israel's largest rehab hospital; has a dedicated speech therapy department. Department-head name not confirmed from primary source; **Shelley Attias** is a confirmed SLP on staff (per ZoomInfo). Page unverified for head identity.
- **Sheba (Tel HaShomer)** — Rehabilitation Hospital is among Israel's top-rated. A third-party source named **"Dr. Minka Hildesheimer"** as Speech Therapy director — **not confirmed on Sheba's own English site**; treat as unconfirmed pending direct verification.
- **Hadassah (Ein Kerem + Mt. Scopus), Rambam (Haifa), Rabin/Beilinson (Petah Tikva)** — All have SLP services within rehab/neurology; no department-head names verified from primary hospital pages.
- No publicly announced clinical-AI SLP partnerships surfaced.

## 6. Regulatory body

- **ISHLA** — Israeli Speech, Hearing and Language Association ([ishla.org.il](https://ishla.org.il/about-us/)), ~1,400 licensed members, IALP-affiliated, professional body (not statutory regulator).
- **Statutory licensing** — **Ministry of Health (Misrad HaBriut)** under the **2008 Regulation of Health Professions Law** (paramedical professions: SLP, OT, PT, dietetics).
- License requirements: recognized degree, 750 hours internship/practicum, Hebrew proficiency (Ulpan Grade 3 or test), MoH knowledge exam; online degrees not recognized.
- No public ISHLA or MoH position on clinical-AI tools located.

## 7. Helsinki Committee / IRB

- Hospital-level Helsinki committees approve studies; **Supreme Helsinki Committee** + MoH Director-General review "special" trials (genetic, ART, etc.).
- Typical timeline **2–4 months**, accelerated 6–10 weeks with templates.
- Required: protocol, Hebrew/Arabic/Russian ICFs, CVs, device safety, **insurance**.
- SLP audio-collection studies are typically non-special and stay at the institutional level, but MoH involvement is triggered for device/diagnostic-AI claims.
- Expect 1–2 revision cycles.

## 8. Pediatric segment

- **Beit Issie Shapiro** (Ra'anana) — broad pediatric disability services.
- **MICHA Haifa & North** (and other MICHA centers) — hearing-impaired children 0–6.
- Pediatric DLD-specific Israeli center not separately verified; many pediatric SLPs operate via Kupot Holim and private clinics.

## 9. AAC in Hebrew

- Hebrew AAC research is active (Tel Aviv University and others have published Hebrew core-vocabulary work in *AAC* journal, 2023–2024).
- **Ezer Mizion** runs a recognized AAC program.
- An Israeli AAC app ("Ola Mundo") was reported in Israel21c.
- **"Saaty AAC" and "Mei-Otzar"** named in prior research were NOT verified through primary sources — **unconfirmed**.

## 10. AphasiaBank protocol (data-collection reference)

Single video-recorded session, audio+video to AphasiaBank guidelines, CHAT transcription. Tasks:
- 2 free-speech elicitations
- 4 picture descriptions
- **Cinderella story retell** (with 10-second prompt rules)
- 1 procedural-discourse task

Tester stays maximally silent / non-verbal encouragement only.
